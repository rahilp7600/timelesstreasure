Green Munia (Amandava formosa)

Location: Mount Abu, Rajasthan, India

This species is found mainly in the dry scrub regions and agricultural lands and is often found close to water. IUCN Status : Vulnerable

Lesser Florican (Sypheotides indicus)

Location: Ajmer, Rajasthan, India

The preferred habitat is grasslands but it sometimes occurs in fields such as those of cotton and lentils. IUCN Status : Critically Endangered

Indian Peafowl (Pavo cristatus)

Location: Gandhinagar, Gujarat, India

It is found in moist and dry-deciduous forests, but has adapted to live in cultivated regions and around human habitations and is usually found where water is available. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Indian Pitta (Pitta brachyura)

Location: Hingolgarh, Gujarat, India

It inhabits scrub jungle, deciduous and dense evergreen forest. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Indian leopard (Panthera pardus fusca)

Location: Jawai, Rajasthan, India

Indian leopards are good climbers and rely on trees for cover, so they are found in various forested habitats, including rainforest, dry deciduous forest, temperate forest, and northern coniferous forest. They are highly adaptable and can be found in diverse tracts across the globe. The Indian leopards are found in the Himalayas, mangroves, and even in the arid regions. IUCN Status : Vulnerable

Wood sandpiper (Tringa glareola)

Location: Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India

As its name suggests, the wood sandpiper favours woodland environments, in particular boreal forest and coniferous woodlands, as well as peatlands and more open areas of swamp and marshland. IUCn Status : Least Concerned

Indian crested porcupine (Hystrix indica) and feral dogs

Location: Little Rann Of Kutch, Gujarat, India

They prefer rocky hillsides, but are also common in tropical and temperate shrublands, grasslands, forests, plantations, and gardens. Here it is captured in the middle of a desert being attacked bu some feral dogs. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Peregrine falcon (Falco peregrinus)

Location: Little Rann Of Kutch, Gujarat, India

They prefer wide-open spaces, and thrive near coasts where shorebirds are common, but they can be found everywhere from tundra to deserts. Peregrines are even known to live on bridges and skyscrapers in major cities. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Painted stork (Mycteria leucocephala)

Location: Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctury, Gujarat, India

They prefer freshwater wetlands in all seasons, but also use irrigation canals and crop fields, particularly flooded rice fields during the monsoon. IUCN Status : Near threatened

Isabelline shrike or Daurian shrike (Lanius isabellinus)

Location: Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctury, Gujarat, India

Like other shrikes it hunts from prominent perches, and impales corpses on thorns or barbed wire as a larder. It breeds in open cultivated country, preferably with thorn bushes. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Sarus crane (Antigone antigone)

Location: Pariej, Gujarat, India

The tallest of the flying birds, standing at a height of up to 1.8 m (5 ft 11 in).They are found in wetlands, marshes, and shallow lakes. IUCN Status : Vulnerable

Indian scops owl (Otus bakkamoena)

Location: Idar, Gujarat, India

The species occurs from eastern Arabia through the Indian Subcontinent, except the far north. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Indian roller (Coracias benghalensis)

Location: Nal Sarovar Bird Sanctury, Gujarat, India

The blue-colored Indian roller habitat mostly consists of a tropical climate, along with deciduous forests and grasslands. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Spotted flycatcher (Muscicapa striata)

Location: Jawai, Rajasthan, India

Spotted flycatchers prefer to spot their prey from high vantage points, so they need a habitat with plenty of tall trees or hedges. They tend to inhabit sparse forests, but also appear in parks, cemeteries or gardens. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Scaly-breasted munia or spotted munia (Lonchura punctulata)

Location: Gujarat, India

They are found mainly on the plains, but can be observed in the foothills of the Himalayas, in which they may be present at altitudes near 2,500 m (1.6 mi), and in the Nilgiris, where they are found at altitudes up to 2,100 m (6,900 ft) during the summer. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Water strider, water skeeter, water scooter, water bug, pond skate, water skipper, water skimmer or puddle flie.(Gerridae)

Location: Thol, Gujarat, India

Gray langur

Location: Thol, Gujarat, India

Little stint (Calidris minuta or Erolia minuta) in breeding plumage

Location: Thol, Gujarat, India

It is a very small wader. It breeds in arctic Europe and Asia, and is a long-distance migrant, wintering south to Africa and south Asia. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Bengal monitor (Varanus bengalensis)

Location: Thol, Gujarat, India

The species is mainly distributed through lower elevations below an altitude of 1500 metres, and is found both in dry semiarid desert habitats to moist forest. They are often found in agricultural areas. IUCN Status : Near Threatened

Pheasant-tailed jacana (Hydrophasianus chirurgus)

Location: Pariej, Gujarat, India

It is found on small to large lakes having sufficient floating vegetation. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Pied bush chat (Saxicola caprata)

Location: Gujarat, India

It is a familiar bird of countryside and open scrub or grassland where it is found perched at the top of short thorn trees or other shrubs, looking out for insect prey. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Red-necked falcon (Falco chicquera)

Location: Little Rann Of Kutch, Gujarat, India

They are found in semi-desert, savannah and other dry open country with some trees, but also riverine forest. IUCN Status : Near Threatened

Eastern imperial eagle (Aquila heliaca)

Location: Little Rann Of Kutch, Gujarat, India

Typical habitats include forest regions in mountains, hills, and along rivers. However, these birds have also been observed living in open landscapes, agricultural areas, steppes, and even semideserts. They prefer open landscapes such as steppes and semi-arid zones. In addition to open habitat, they also require trees for nesting, though this need can be fulfilled by a small group of trees or even a single tree. IUCN Status : Vulnerable

Red spurfowl (Galloperdix spadicea)

Location: Mount Abu, Rajasthan, India

The species is found in scrub, dry and moist-deciduous forests often in hilly country. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

White-crowned sparrow (Zonotrichia leucophrys)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

Inhabit brushy areas, they can also be found in thickets, fields, gardens, and urban areas. Their breeding habitat includes the taiga, tundra, alpine meadows, and woodland edges. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Eastern bluebird (Sialia sialis)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

The habitat consists of open forest or woodland with little understory and ground cover. In North America, these birds are most commonly found in burned jack pine forests, clear-cut deciduous woods, orchards, swamps, and longleaf pine ecosystems. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Eastern bluebird (Sialia sialis)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

The habitat consists of open forest or woodland with little understory and ground cover. In North America, these birds are most commonly found in burned jack pine forests, clear-cut deciduous woods, orchards, swamps, and longleaf pine ecosystems. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Rusty blackbird (Euphagus carolinus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They usually nest at the edge of ponds and wetlands with the cup nest located in a tree or dense shrub, often over the water. Emerging dragonflies and their larvae are important food items during the summer. IUCN Status : Vulnerable

Common starling (Sturnus vulgaris)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They prefer urban or suburban areas, reedbeds, grassy areas such as farmland, grazing pastures, playing fields, golf courses, and airfields where short grass makes foraging easy. They occasionally inhabit open forests and woodlands and are sometimes found in shrubby areas. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Red-bellied woodpecker ( Melanerpes carolinus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They are non-migratory and usually inhabit deciduous forests near rivers and streams. These birds can also be found in plantations, gardens, groves, and urban areas. Red-bellied woodpeckers are adaptable to a wide range of forested habitats. Though they’re mostly related to mature hardwood forests, in addition, they thrive in blended pine-hardwood forests, mesic pine Flatwoods, closely timbered bottomlands, swampy woods, and riparian forests. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Great horned owl (Bubo virginianus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They favor dense forest near open areas, habitat often shared by their daytime counterparts, Red-tailed Hawks. It also includes agricultural areas, tropical rainforests, mountainous areas, mangrove swamp forests, rocky coasts, saltwater marshes, freshwater marshes, mesquite, semi-arid grasslands, cacti deserts, deciduous, mixed and coniferous forests. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

House finch (Haemorhous mexicanus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

Open grasslands, deserts, prairies, savannah and coniferous forests all support the feeding and nesting needs of the species. House finches inhabit urban and suburban areas, as well as various semi-open areas. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Hooded merganser (Lophodytes cucullatus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

For preference the hooded merganser lives on small bodies of water such as ponds and small estuaries where there is ample emergent aquatic vegetation, but it also inhabits larger wetlands, impoundments, flooded timber, and rivers. They prefer fresh water but do occur on brackish water bodies as well. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Gadwall (Mareca strepera)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They breed mainly in the Great Plains and prairies. On migration and in winter, look for Gadwall in reservoirs, ponds, fresh and salt water marshes, city parks, sewage ponds, or muddy edges of estuaries. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

American goldfinch (Spinus tristis)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

The American goldfinch prefers open country where weeds thrive, such as fields, meadows, flood plains, as well as roadsides, orchards, and gardens. It may also be found in open deciduous and riparian woodlands and areas of secondary growth. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Long-eared owl (Asio otus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

Long-eared owls inhabit dense woodlands with coniferous or broadleaved trees, often along streams or wetlands. They also need adjacent open areas, such as grasslands, old fields, or desert oases, where they hunt for small rodents, birds, and insects. They roost in dense stands of evergreens or vine-covered thickets. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Long-eared owl (Asio otus)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

Dark-eyed junco (Junco hyemalis)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

In winter, open woods, undergrowth, roadsides, brush. Over its wide range, breeding habitat is consistently coniferous or mixed woodland, usually in rather open situations such as edges or clearings. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Chipping sparrow (Spizella passerina)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

Inhabits in open woodlands, orchards, suburban areas, farms. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Palm warbler (Setophaga palmarum)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

They can also be found in brushy thickets, weedy fields, forest edges, fence rows, savannas and dune habitat. They’ll often be found on or near the ground, poking through weedy pockets looking for berries as well as insects. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

Scarlet tanager (Piranga olivacea)

Location: Central Park, NYC, New York, USA

The primary habitat of the Scarlet Tanager is a mixed deciduous forest interspersed with hemlocks and pines. These birds inhabit large stretches of deciduous forest, especially with oaks, across eastern North America. Scarlet Tanagers also inhabit urban parks and suburban areas with large trees. IUCN Status : Least Concerned

American painted lady or American lady (Vanessa virginiensis)

Location: Prospect Park, NYC, New York, USA
